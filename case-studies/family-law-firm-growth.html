The Challenge

A family law firm serving Toronto and the surrounding GTA had been operating for over a decade, building a loyal referral network. But referrals alone were no longer enough. The firm's website was generating fewer than 40 organic visits per month, and it had no presence in AI-powered search results — the channel increasingly used by people navigating divorce, custody disputes, and separation agreements.

The firm was consistently losing prospective clients to larger, better-optimised competitors who dominated page one for queries like "Toronto family lawyer" and "divorce lawyer near me." More critically, the firm had no after-hours coverage. When a prospective client visited the website at 10pm on a Sunday in the midst of a separation crisis, there was nothing to capture that lead — no chatbot, no online booking, no immediate response mechanism.

The managing partner estimated the firm was losing between 8 and 12 potential consultations per month simply because enquiries sent after business hours went unanswered for 12 to 18 hours. In the emotionally charged, time-sensitive world of family law, that delay often meant the client had already called a competitor.

The Strategy

LexScale.ai conducted a full digital audit covering the firm's existing website architecture, keyword coverage, competitor positioning, and AI search visibility. The audit revealed three core gaps: thin topical coverage across family law practice areas, no structured data markup, and zero Google Business Profile optimisation.

The strategy was built around three pillars:

1. AI SEO Content Architecture

Build a topical authority cluster covering all family law practice areas — divorce, separation, child custody, spousal support, property division, and adoption — with entity-rich content designed to be cited by Google AI Overviews and ChatGPT.

2. Google Business Profile Optimisation

Fully optimise the GBP listing with updated service descriptions, practice area categories, regular posts, and a structured Q&A section targeting the firm's highest-converting query types.

3. AI Chatbot Deployment

Deploy a trained AI chatbot that could handle initial enquiries 24/7, pre-qualify prospective clients based on practice area and urgency, and book consultation appointments directly into the lawyers' calendars.

Implementation

In the first month, LexScale.ai rebuilt the firm's website content architecture. The existing five-page website was expanded into a comprehensive hub-and-spoke structure with a family law overview page linking to 12 dedicated practice area pages. Every page was written to answer the specific questions prospective clients type into Google and AI assistants — "how is property divided in an Ontario divorce," "what rights do fathers have in custody disputes in Ontario," and similar high-intent queries.

Structured data (FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, and LegalService schema) was applied across every page, making the content eligible for Google rich results and improving the probability of AI citation. The GBP listing was transformed from a basic directory entry into an active marketing asset with weekly posts and complete service coverage.

In month two, the AI chatbot went live. It was trained on the firm's practice areas, intake process, and fee structure. The chatbot's primary function was to engage website visitors immediately, understand their situation, and route them toward booking a consultation — handling the entire intake conversation in plain, empathetic language appropriate for someone navigating a family breakdown.

The firm's website began appearing in Google AI Overviews for local family law queries within 10 weeks of the content launch. ChatGPT began citing the firm's educational content on Ontario divorce law and child custody when answering related questions from Ontario users.

Perhaps most significantly, the after-hours lead capture problem was eliminated entirely. The AI chatbot handled 38% of total consultation bookings across the six-month period — the majority of those conversations initiated outside business hours. The firm's dependency on referrals was reduced from roughly 80% of new clients to below 50%, creating a scalable, predictable client acquisition channel for the first time in the firm's history.
