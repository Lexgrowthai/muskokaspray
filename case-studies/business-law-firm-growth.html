The Challenge

A mid-size Ontario corporate and business law firm with 8 lawyers and 15 years of practice had virtually no content strategy and zero AI search presence. Despite deep expertise in M&A, commercial contracts, employment law, and corporate governance, the firm was invisible in ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Perplexity responses. High-value corporate clients were finding competitors with stronger online authority — firms that had invested in content and entity SEO. The managing partner described the situation as 'competing with one hand tied behind our back.'

The Strategy

LexScale.ai designed a full-stack AI authority strategy built around topical depth and entity recognition. The core insight: AI search systems cite firms that have established themselves as authoritative entities within specific legal domains. This required two parallel tracks — building content authority through a 5-silo content architecture covering every major practice area, and building entity authority by ensuring every AI system could accurately identify the firm, its lawyers, and its expertise areas through structured data and entity signals.

Implementation

Month 1: Organization schema, Attorney schema for all 8 lawyers, and LocalBusiness schema deployed across the entire site. FAQPage schema added to all 14 practice area pages. Month 1–3: 47 articles published across 5 content silos — M&A (10 articles), commercial contracts (9), employment law (10), real estate law (9), corporate governance (9). Each article optimised for AI citation with direct-answer introductions, FAQ sections, and entity-rich language. Month 3–6: AI chatbot deployed with corporate intake flow. Google Business Profile updated with corporate service descriptions. Month 6–8: Link-building and authority signal amplification targeting legal directories and industry publications.

The Results

By month 8, the firm was cited by ChatGPT for 23 corporate law queries, appeared in Google AI Overviews for 11 practice area searches, and was cited in Perplexity for 9 queries. Organic traffic grew 445% over the engagement period. Three major corporate client mandates were directly attributed to AI search visibility — prospects who mentioned finding the firm through 'a ChatGPT recommendation' or 'Google AI.' The firm's AI search authority now rivals firms with 10× the marketing budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions About This Case Study

How long does it take to establish AI search authority?
For a corporate law firm starting from zero AI presence, 6–9 months is a realistic timeline to achieve consistent citations across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity. The first citations typically appear within 60–90 days for the most specific long-tail queries; authority across broader queries builds over 6+ months as topical depth accumulates.
What is a content silo and why does it matter for AI search?
A content silo is a cluster of related content built around a central topic — a hub page and 8–12 supporting articles covering every aspect of that topic. AI search systems favour firms that demonstrate comprehensive expertise in a subject area over firms with one or two isolated pages. Five content silos covering your practice areas signals to AI systems that your firm is the definitive source on those legal topics.
How does entity SEO differ from traditional keyword SEO?
Traditional keyword SEO optimises for what words appear on a page. Entity SEO builds a machine-readable identity for your firm that AI systems can recognise and cite. This includes Organization schema (your firm as an entity), Attorney schema (your lawyers as entities), structured data connecting your firm to specific legal services and locations, and signals that help AI disambiguate your firm from other similarly named entities.
Is AI search relevant for corporate and B2B law firms?
Yes — and it may be more valuable for B2B law firms than consumer-facing ones. Corporate clients, in-house counsel, and business owners increasingly use ChatGPT and Perplexity to identify legal expertise before making contact. A single corporate mandate in M&A or commercial litigation can be worth $50,000–$500,000+. Being cited in AI responses for corporate legal queries is one of the highest-ROI marketing positions available to a business law firm.
