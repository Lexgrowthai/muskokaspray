The Challenge

A mid-size criminal defence firm in Ontario was well-regarded within the legal community but virtually invisible online. Despite years of successful case outcomes and strong word-of-mouth referrals, the firm's website ranked beyond page three for every major criminal defence keyword in its city. More alarmingly, when prospective clients turned to ChatGPT or Google AI Overviews for help finding a criminal lawyer, this firm was never mentioned.

The digital landscape for criminal defence in Ontario is intensely competitive. Aggregator sites, legal directories, and large multi-location firms with dedicated SEO budgets dominate the first page of Google. Traditional pay-per-click advertising is extraordinarily expensive — cost-per-click for "criminal lawyer Toronto" regularly exceeds $80. The firm had tried PPC previously and found the return insufficient to justify continued investment.

The core problem was thin, generic content. The firm's website had a single "Criminal Defence" service page covering everything from DUI to fraud to assault — without the depth, specificity, or structured data that search engines and AI systems require to identify a site as a genuine topical authority. As a result, AI systems had no reliable entity signal to draw on when generating answers about criminal lawyers in Ontario.

The Strategy

LexScale.ai diagnosed the firm's primary weakness as an entity SEO deficit. The firm existed as a named entity in the real world but had not been properly established as an entity in the digital knowledge graph. The strategy focused on three interconnected objectives:

1. Entity Establishment

Build consistent entity signals across the firm's website, Google Business Profile, legal directories (Justia, Avvo, Martindale-Hubbell, the Law Society of Ontario), and structured data. Ensure the firm's name, address, phone number, practice areas, and credentials were identical and machine-readable across every platform.

2. Topical Authority Content Cluster

Replace the single generic service page with a comprehensive content cluster: a criminal defence hub page linking to 16 dedicated offence-specific pages covering DUI/impaired driving, assault, domestic assault, drug offences, fraud, theft, weapons offences, sexual offences, bail hearings, and more. Each page was built to answer the specific questions a person charged with that offence would ask.

3. Structured Data Markup

Implement FAQPage, LegalService, BreadcrumbList, and Organization JSON-LD schema across the entire site — giving AI systems the machine-readable context needed to cite the firm confidently in response to Ontario criminal defence queries.

Implementation

The first two weeks were dedicated entirely to entity foundation work: auditing and correcting all directory listings, establishing consistent NAP (name, address, phone) data across 22 platforms, and implementing Organization and LegalService schema on the firm's homepage and key service pages.

Over weeks three through eight, LexScale.ai's legal content team produced the full topical cluster. Each offence-specific page was written to a minimum of 1,800 words, structured to answer the questions that Ontario residents actually ask when facing a criminal charge: what happens at a bail hearing, what are the penalties for impaired driving in Ontario, what should you say to police after an arrest, and similar high-intent queries.

Every page received FAQPage schema with four to six question-answer pairs drawn directly from the most common queries on that topic. The hub-and-spoke internal linking structure was built to pass authority from the main criminal defence page to every offence page and back — creating a self-reinforcing topical authority signal that both Google and AI systems could readily interpret.
