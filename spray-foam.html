Why Spray Foam

Superior in Every Way

Spray polyurethane foam outperforms every conventional insulation type across every metric that matters.

⚡

Energy Savings Up to 40%

Closed-cell SPF achieves R-6.5 per inch — the highest R-value of any commercially available insulation material.

🌬️

Complete Air Sealing

SPF expands to fill every crack and void, creating an impenetrable air barrier that reduces HVAC load significantly.

💧

Moisture & Vapour Barrier

Closed-cell foam acts as a Class II vapour retarder, protecting your structure from moisture damage, mould, and rot.

🔇

Acoustic Insulation

Dramatically reduces noise transmission between spaces — ideal for manufacturing, healthcare, and commercial environments.

🏗️

Structural Reinforcement

Closed-cell SPF bonds to substrates, increasing racking strength and improving overall structural integrity of walls and roofs.

📅

25+ Year Lifespan

Unlike traditional insulation, spray foam will not sag, settle, or degrade over time — delivering consistent performance for decades.

  • Pharmaceutical cold rooms
  • Agricultural storage

FAQs

Common Questions

Closed-cell SPF is denser, has a higher R-value (R-6.5/inch), acts as a vapour barrier, and adds structural strength. Open-cell is softer, more affordable, and offers excellent sound dampening — but has a lower R-value and is not a vapour barrier. We recommend closed-cell for most commercial and industrial applications due to its superior performance.

Project timelines vary based on scope. A typical 10,000 sq ft warehouse roof deck takes 1–2 days. Larger industrial projects are phased to minimize operational disruption. We provide detailed project schedules upfront and work around your operational needs whenever possible.

Yes. All our spray foam systems are tested and listed by Underwriters Laboratories of Canada (ULC) and comply with the National Building Code of Canada. Our certified technicians ensure every installation meets applicable OBC and NBC requirements, and we provide full documentation for building permit purposes.

SPF application requires temporary evacuation of the immediate work area during spraying and for a short cure period (typically 2–4 hours). We work closely with facility managers to phase work schedules and minimize business disruption. Proper ventilation is maintained throughout all operations.

Most commercial clients see a return on investment within 3–7 years through reduced energy costs alone. Additional savings come from reduced HVAC equipment sizing, improved building durability, lower maintenance costs, and potential energy rebates from provincial utility programs.
