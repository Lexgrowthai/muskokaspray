Why Intumescent Coating

The Gold Standard in
Passive Fire Protection

Intumescent coatings provide certified passive fire protection while maintaining the aesthetic appeal of exposed structural steel.

🔥

Up to 4-Hour Fire Rating

Code Compliant

All systems are ULC, UL, and NFPA tested and certified, meeting and exceeding National Building Code requirements.

Minimal Added Weight

Architectural Finish

Available in a range of decorative topcoats, intumescent coatings allow exposed structural steel to remain a design feature.

Long-Term Durability

Our Method

The Application Process

🔍
Step 01

Surface Preparation

All steel is cleaned, primed, and inspected to ensure proper adhesion and substrate compatibility for the coating system.

🖌️
Step 02

Primer Application

Anti-corrosion primer is applied to protect the steel substrate and ensure maximum adhesion of the intumescent system.

🔥
Step 03

Intumescent Coating

Intumescent material is applied in calculated thickness using airless spray equipment to achieve the specified fire rating.

✅
Step 04

Topcoat & Inspection

Decorative or protective topcoat is applied, followed by a thorough inspection and complete documentation package delivery.

FAQs

Common Questions

Intumescent coating is a passive fire protection material that, when exposed to high temperatures (typically above 200°C), undergoes a chemical reaction causing it to expand to 50–100 times its original thickness. This expanded, insulating char layer protects the structural steel beneath, preventing it from reaching critical failure temperatures during a fire event.

Our certified systems can achieve fire resistance ratings of 30 minutes, 1 hour, 1.5 hours, 2 hours, and up to 4 hours depending on the steel section factor, the specific product used, and the applied thickness. We calculate required thickness for each structural member based on the ULC/UL listing and engineering requirements.

Yes. Intumescent coatings are well-suited for retrofit applications. We assess existing primer and coating conditions, prepare the substrate as needed, and apply the intumescent system. This is commonly required when buildings undergo change-of-use, occupancy upgrades, or when previous fire protection has deteriorated.

Yes — we offer both interior and exterior-grade intumescent systems. Exterior formulations are specifically engineered to resist moisture, UV exposure, and weathering while maintaining their fire protection capability. Appropriate primers and topcoats are selected based on the environment, ensuring long-term performance.

Interior intumescent coatings, when properly applied and protected by a topcoat, can last the lifetime of the building without significant maintenance. Exterior systems may require periodic inspection and touch-up. We provide a maintenance guide and recommend annual visual inspections to ensure the coating remains intact and undamaged.
