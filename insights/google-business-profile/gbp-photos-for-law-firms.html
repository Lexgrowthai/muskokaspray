Google Business Profile Photos for Law Firms: What to Post and Why It Matters

GBP photos directly affect how often your law firm appears in Google Maps and AI results. Learn exactly which photos to post, how often to update them, and what the algorithm rewards.

LexScale.ai Editorial·July 2026·8 min read

Your Google Business Profile photos are doing more work than you probably realise. Google's local algorithm actively considers photo quantity, recency, and diversity when ranking businesses in Maps and the local pack. And for AI platforms that use Google's local data — including Gemini, which integrates directly with Google services — your GBP photos contribute to the overall trust and engagement signals that influence whether you appear in AI-generated recommendations.

Why Photos Are a Local SEO Ranking Factor

Google has confirmed that businesses with photos receive significantly more clicks and engagement than those without. Businesses with more than 100 photos receive 520% more phone calls, 2,717% more direction requests, and 1,065% more website clicks than the average business, according to Google's own data. For law firms competing in local search, this engagement data feeds directly into ranking signals.

The recency of photos also matters. A profile that had 20 photos uploaded three years ago and nothing since signals an inactive, possibly outdated business. A profile that adds two or three photos per month signals an active, engaged business — and active, engaged businesses rank better in local search. Make photo updates a monthly discipline, not a one-time task.

The Exact Photos Your Law Firm GBP Needs

Exterior Photos: Help People Find You

Upload at least three exterior shots: the building facade, the entrance sign, and the parking area or street view. These help clients identify your office when they arrive and signal to Google that your location information is accurate and verified. Include photos at different times of day and in different seasons — this keeps your profile fresh and helps clients find you in different lighting conditions.

Interior Photos: Build Trust Before They Visit

Your reception area, conference room, and individual offices all contribute to trust building. A warm, well-maintained office signals a professional, stable firm. A cramped, dated-looking space can undermine the confidence you have spent thousands building with your website. Make sure the interior photos reflect the actual client experience — if your conference room is where you meet clients, that room should look impressive in photos.

Team Photos: Humanise Your Firm

Prospective clients hire attorneys, not logos. Photos of your attorneys — professional headshots and candid working shots — make your firm feel human and accessible. Group team photos convey a sense of community and shared purpose. Avoid the stiff, formal courtroom-pose photos that every firm has. Capture attorneys working at their desks, in conversation with colleagues, in team meetings. Real moments build real connection.

Logo and Cover Photo: Brand Consistency

Your logo photo and cover photo appear prominently in search results and should be high-resolution, on-brand, and consistent with your website design. Mismatched branding between your GBP and your website creates subtle cognitive dissonance that reduces trust. Keep them aligned.

Photo Naming and Metadata

When you upload photos to your GBP, rename the files with descriptive, keyword-rich names before uploading: "personal-injury-law-firm-chicago-reception.jpg" is better than "IMG_4821.jpg." While Google's systems process image content rather than relying solely on filenames, descriptive filenames reinforce the contextual signals that help your profile appear in relevant local searches.

Connecting GBP Photos to AI Search

Gemini integrates directly with Google's local business data, meaning your GBP profile — including photos, reviews, and category information — influences Gemini's responses to local legal queries. A well-maintained, actively updated GBP with strong photo engagement sends trust signals that carry into AI search recommendations. Combine strong photos with excellent AI SEO practices and your local visibility across both Google Maps and AI platforms compounds significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many photos should a law firm have on their Google Business Profile?
Google recommends a minimum of 10 photos, but law firms with 50 or more consistently outperform those with fewer in local pack rankings. Aim for fresh photos monthly — recency is weighted. Cover multiple categories: exterior, interior, team, logo, and at-work photos. Profiles with active, recent photo libraries get 35% more direction requests and 42% more website clicks than those without.
Do stock photos hurt a law firm's Google Business Profile?
Yes. Google's algorithm recognises and devalues stock photography because it signals inauthenticity. Real photos of your actual office, actual attorneys, and actual team members dramatically outperform stock images for engagement and trust signals. Even modest smartphone photos of your real environment outperform polished stock shots. Authenticity is the currency of local search.
Should law firms respond to photos posted by others on their GBP?
You cannot delete or moderate user-uploaded photos unless they violate Google's policies. You can flag inappropriate content for removal. The best strategy is to post so many high-quality photos of your own that user photos represent a small fraction of your profile's visual content.
