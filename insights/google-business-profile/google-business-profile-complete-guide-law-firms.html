Google Business Profile for Law Firms: The Complete 2026 Guide

Your Google Business Profile is the single most important local marketing asset your law firm owns. Done right, it drives calls, builds trust, and feeds directly into Gemini AI recommendations. Here's the complete playbook.

June 23, 2026
14 min read
LexScale.ai Editorial

Why Your Google Business Profile Matters More Than Ever in 2026

In 2026, Google Business Profile data feeds directly into Google Gemini AI summaries. When someone asks Gemini "best family lawyer in Toronto" or "criminal defence lawyer near me," Gemini pulls from GBP data — your review score, your listed practice areas, your description, your activity. A well-optimized GBP doesn't just help you appear in the local pack (the map results at the top of search); it feeds the AI recommendation layer that's increasingly where legal decisions begin.

Local search is 46% of all Google searches. For law firms, local intent is even higher — people looking for lawyers are almost always looking for lawyers in their geography. Whether someone is searching for a family lawyer in Barrie, a real estate lawyer in Mississauga, or a criminal defence lawyer in Ottawa, they are conducting a local search. Your GBP is the front door to your firm for all of these searchers.

The local 3-pack — the map with three business listings that appears at the top of local search results — is prime real estate. Studies consistently show that the top 3 local results capture over 75% of clicks for local searches. For a law firm, appearing in the 3-pack for "family lawyer [city]" or "personal injury lawyer near me" can mean the difference between a thriving referral pipeline and an empty phone.

Your Google Business Profile also powers your Knowledge Panel — the information box that appears on the right side of Google search results when someone searches specifically for your firm name. This is the first thing a referred prospect sees when they Google you after getting your card or your name from a friend. A complete, well-reviewed Knowledge Panel builds instant credibility.

And in 2026, the connection between GBP and Gemini AI overviews has become direct. Gemini references GBP review scores and descriptions when generating local legal recommendations. Law firms with incomplete profiles or low review counts are being passed over in Gemini responses in favour of competitors with complete, active profiles. If you want to appear in AI search — not just traditional search — your GBP is ground zero.

The good news: most law firms have dramatically under-optimized GBP profiles. The majority have incomplete descriptions, too few photos, no posts, and no systematic review acquisition strategy. This means the opportunity for competitive differentiation through GBP optimization is still significant, even in competitive markets. The firms that invest in GBP now will hold significant advantages as AI-powered local search matures.

Setting Up Your Google Business Profile: Claim and Verify

Before you can optimize your GBP, you need to own it. Many law firms already have a GBP listing — created by Google automatically from public data, or by a previous employee or marketing vendor. The first step is to search for your firm name on Google Maps to check whether a listing already exists.

If a listing exists and is unclaimed, you'll see a "Claim this business" option. Click it and follow the verification process. If a listing exists and is claimed by someone you no longer have access to (a former employee, a marketing agency), you'll need to request ownership transfer through Google's business profile support process — which requires verifying your association with the business.

If no listing exists, go to business.google.com and click "Add your business." You'll be prompted to enter your business name, category, location, contact information, and service areas. For law firms with a physical office, always enter your actual office address — this is required for local pack eligibility. If you operate a virtual or mobile practice, you can hide your address and enter service areas instead, but this significantly reduces local pack eligibility.

Primary category selection is one of the most important decisions in your GBP setup. The most commonly used primary category for law firms is "Attorney" — and it has the broadest search volume coverage. However, Google also has more specific categories including "Law Firm," "Family Law Attorney," "Criminal Justice Attorney," "Personal Injury Attorney," "Immigration Attorney," "Real Estate Attorney," and others. Use the most specific category that accurately describes your primary practice area. If you do primarily family law, "Family Law Attorney" as your primary category sends a stronger signal than generic "Attorney."

Beyond your primary category, you can add up to 10 secondary categories. Use them to cover all your significant practice areas. A firm that does family law, real estate, and wills & estates should have all three reflected in secondary categories. Secondary categories expand the pool of searches you're eligible to appear for.

Verification is the final setup step. Google offers several verification methods: postcard (mailed to your business address, takes 5-14 days, contains a PIN you enter online), phone (immediate, if available for your account), email, or video verification (newer — you walk through your office on video call with a Google reviewer). Once verified, your listing becomes eligible for local pack rankings.

Common issues to watch for: duplicate listings (multiple GBP listings for the same location — report extras for removal), address format consistency (your address must be formatted identically across your GBP, your website, and all directories — "Suite 200" vs "#200" vs "Unit 200" creates NAP inconsistency that damages rankings), and suspended listings (usually caused by keyword-stuffing in business names, which violates Google's terms).

Optimizing Every Field in Your GBP

A complete GBP profile outperforms an incomplete one in local rankings. Google's algorithm treats completeness as a quality signal. Here's how to optimize every field that matters.

Business name: Use your exact registered business name — nothing more, nothing less. Do not add keywords, location descriptors, or practice area terms. "Smith & Associates Family Law Toronto" when your registered name is "Smith & Associates" is a violation that can lead to listing suspension. Keywords go in your description, services, and posts — not your name.

Primary phone number: Use a local phone number rather than a toll-free 800 number wherever possible. Local numbers reinforce geographic relevance, which is a local ranking signal. If you have both, list the local number as primary.

Website URL: Don't just link to your homepage. Link to your most important practice area page — the one you most want to rank for locally. If family law is your primary revenue driver, link to your family law service page. This passes link equity to your most important page and aligns GBP with your priority SEO goals.

Business hours: Keep them accurate and current. Google will flag businesses as "often busy" or "usually quiet" based on visit patterns, but inaccurate hours damage trust when clients show up and you're closed. Add holiday hours for statutory holidays — Google prompts you to do this automatically.

Business description: You have 750 characters. Use all of them. This is one of the most underutilized fields in law firm GBPs. A great description covers: your primary practice areas (named explicitly), the geographic areas you serve, your years of experience, key differentiators (initial consultation offering, language capabilities, specialized certifications), and a human tone that builds trust. Do not include promotional language, pricing, or URLs — Google rejects these. Write for both the human reader and Google's local ranking algorithm, which indexes your description for keyword relevance.

Services section: Add every practice area as a service with a 300-character description. This creates a structured, indexed catalogue of your capabilities that feeds both traditional local search and AI recommendations.

Attributes: These are the checkboxes — veteran-owned, women-led, LGBTQ+ friendly, wheelchair accessible, languages spoken. Complete every applicable attribute. These appear prominently in your listing and can be the deciding factor for clients who specifically prioritize these characteristics.

GBP Photos: The Underrated Ranking Factor

Photos are one of the most consistently underutilized elements of law firm Google Business Profiles. Most law firms have a logo, maybe one exterior shot, and nothing else. This is a significant missed opportunity — both for rankings and for client conversion.

GBP profiles with 10 or more photos receive 42% more direction requests and 35% more website clicks than profiles with fewer photos. The mechanism is both algorithmic (Google treats photo activity as an engagement signal) and psychological (more photos = more credible, more established, more trustworthy).

The minimum photo set every law firm needs: an exterior photo (so clients can identify your building when they arrive), an interior photo showing your reception or waiting area, at least one team photo showing lawyers and staff, your logo in the correct dimensions, and a cover photo that makes a strong visual first impression. Beyond this minimum, the target is 10+ photos, updated regularly.

The most effective photo types for law firms are team headshots in professional attire, candid shots of lawyers in consultation or working (without showing client faces or identifiable documents), reception and waiting areas that signal professionalism, meeting rooms, office exterior in daylight, and community involvement photos if your firm does charitable work.

What to avoid: stock photos. Google can often detect stock images, and even when it can't, stock photos signal inauthenticity to potential clients who are trying to assess whether your firm is real and established. Low-resolution images are also counterproductive — they signal poor attention to detail. Minimum recommended size is 720×720px; ideal is 1200×900px or larger for cover photos.

Best practice: add one to two new photos every month. Photo recency is a signal to Google that your profile is active. A profile with 20 photos added two years ago will underperform a profile with 15 photos updated consistently over the past year. Build monthly photo updates into your marketing calendar.

In 2026, Google Gemini AI increasingly references visual content from GBP profiles when generating local business recommendations. A rich photo library improves the likelihood that Gemini will include visual references to your firm in AI overview responses for local legal searches. This is a relatively new competitive dimension that most law firms have not yet capitalized on.

Google Reviews: Your Most Powerful GBP Signal

Reviews are the single most powerful signal in your Google Business Profile. A firm with 50 reviews at 4.8 stars almost always outperforms a firm with 5 reviews at 5.0 stars in local pack rankings. Volume and recency matter as much as rating — Google's algorithm treats a consistent stream of recent reviews as evidence of an active, trusted business.

The strategy for accumulating genuine reviews starts with the ask. The best time to ask a client for a review is immediately after a successful outcome — when the matter is resolved and the client is grateful. For litigation matters, that's the day of verdict or settlement. For real estate, the day of closing. For family law, when the final order is issued. The emotional state of the client in that moment is exactly right for a sincere, enthusiastic review.

The most effective ask is personal: a phone call or in-person conversation, not an automated email blast. "I'm so glad we were able to achieve this outcome for you. If you have two minutes, a Google review would genuinely help other families in similar situations find our firm — I can send you the direct link right now." Then send the link. Follow up once by email three days later if no review has been posted.

Your direct Google review link is available in your GBP dashboard under "Share profile." Shorten it and send it directly in your follow-up email, hyperlinked as "Leave us a Google review." Reducing friction dramatically increases conversion.

Regarding Law Society of Ontario rules: you can ask clients for reviews, you cannot offer incentives (no gifts, fee discounts, or other consideration), you cannot write reviews yourself or have staff, family members, or friends post reviews about your practice, and you must not solicit reviews from clients in a way that creates undue influence. The compliant approach is simple: ask satisfied former clients for their honest opinion. The LSO takes misleading advertising seriously, and a pattern of fake or incentivized reviews creates real professional risk.

Responding to reviews matters as much as generating them. Respond to every review — positive and negative — within 48 hours. For positive reviews, thank the reviewer by name (if they used their name), reference the type of matter handled (without revealing confidential details), and include a natural keyword in your response. For negative reviews: respond professionally, acknowledge the frustration, never reveal case details or argue publicly, and take the conversation offline by providing a direct contact. Potential clients who read a professional response to a negative review often come away with more confidence in your firm than if you had no negative reviews at all.

GBP Posts, Q&A, and Advanced Features

Most law firms set up their GBP profile and never return to it. This is a significant missed opportunity. Google rewards active profiles with better local rankings — and there are several underutilized features that drive both rankings and direct client engagement.

GBP Posts are short updates (up to 1,500 characters) that appear in your Knowledge Panel and in the local pack. There are four types: What's New (general updates, expire after 7 days), Events (tied to a date and time, expire on event date), Offers (promotional posts, expire on set date), and Updates (same as What's New in most markets). Law firms should post at least weekly. Content that works well: legal tips relevant to your practice area, firm news and announcements, community involvement, notable verdicts or settlements (appropriately anonymized), and educational content about the legal process. Each post is an indexed piece of content that can appear in search results and in Gemini AI local overviews.

The Q&A section is one of the most overlooked features of GBP. Anyone — including your competitors — can submit questions, and anyone can answer them. Monitor your Q&A section weekly. When you answer as the business owner, your answer appears first and is marked as the business's official response. Pre-populate your Q&A with the questions your clients most commonly ask: "Do you offer free consultations?", "What areas of law does your firm practice?", "What is your office's parking situation?", "Do you offer payment plans?" — then answer them thoroughly. This not only controls the narrative but creates indexed FAQ content that feeds AI search.

GBP Messaging allows potential clients to send your firm a direct message through Google Search or Maps. Enable this feature and ensure someone monitors it — unanswered messages signal poor responsiveness to Google's algorithm and to potential clients browsing your listing. Response time is displayed publicly on your profile.

If your firm uses scheduling software — Calendly, Clio Grow, or similar — connect it to your GBP via the Booking button feature. This allows potential clients to book a consultation directly from your Google listing without ever visiting your website. Reducing friction in the conversion path measurably increases booking rates.

The Products and Services section should list all your practice areas with descriptions and, where appropriate, price ranges or starting fees. This structured data is indexed by Google and creates additional keyword coverage for your profile. A family law firm might list: "Divorce — contested and uncontested," "Child Custody and Access," "Child Support," "Spousal Support," "Property Division," and "Family Law Mediation," each with a 2-3 sentence description of what that service involves.

Taken together, a fully optimized GBP — complete profile, regular photos, weekly posts, active Q&A management, messaging enabled, booking connected, and a consistent review acquisition strategy — positions your law firm as the clear choice in local search. In a market where most firms barely maintain their listings, this level of investment creates durable competitive advantage that compounds over time.
