Choosing the Right Google Business Profile Categories for Your Law Firm

Wrong GBP categories can tank your local rankings. Learn which primary and secondary categories actually help law firms rank higher in Google Maps and AI-powered local results.

LexScale.ai Editorial·July 2026·8 min read

Your Google Business Profile primary category is one of the most powerful local SEO levers available to law firms — and one of the most frequently misconfigured. The primary category tells Google what type of business you are at a fundamental level. Get it right, and you are more likely to appear in searches for your exact type of service. Get it wrong, and you are competing in the wrong category while missing the searches that matter most.

Understanding Google's Category System for Law Firms

Google's category system is hierarchical. At the broadest level, a firm is a "Law Firm." Within that, there are specific attorney types: Personal Injury Attorney, Criminal Justice Attorney, Family Law Attorney, Estate Planning Attorney, Immigration Attorney, Employment Attorney, Real Estate Attorney, Divorce Lawyer, Corporate Attorney, Bankruptcy Attorney, and others. Each category influences which local searches your profile appears in.

The relationship between category and search visibility is not always linear. In some markets, "Personal Injury Attorney" as a primary category will outperform "Law Firm" for personal injury searches because Google interprets the specific category as a stronger relevance signal. In other markets, "Law Firm" with multiple specific secondary categories performs better. Testing and monitoring your rankings after category changes is the only way to know what works in your specific market.

Primary Category Recommendations by Practice Type

Personal Injury Firms

Use "Personal Injury Attorney" as your primary category if personal injury is your dominant practice. Add secondary categories for specific injury types your firm handles: Auto Accidents → look for available sub-categories, or use "Lawyer" as a broader catch-all alongside specific ones. Also add "Law Firm" as a secondary. This specific categorisation helps you rank for "personal injury attorney near me" searches, which are often the highest-value local queries for this practice type.

Multi-Practice Firms

If your firm handles multiple practice areas with no single dominant focus, "Law Firm" is typically the right primary category. Then stack your secondary categories to cover each major practice area: Family Law Attorney, Criminal Justice Attorney, Employment Attorney, Estate Planning Attorney, Real Estate Attorney. This broad-plus-specific approach gives you local visibility across multiple practice areas.

Criminal Defense Firms

"Criminal Justice Attorney" is the specific Google category for criminal defense practices. Use it as your primary category if criminal defense represents the majority of your caseload. Secondary categories can include DUI Attorney (search for the exact Google category name), Drug Offense Lawyer (if available), and Law Firm as a general secondary. Criminal defense is highly time-sensitive and high-stakes — ranking well in local search for these terms is directly tied to case volume.

How Categories Interact With AI Search

Gemini and other AI platforms that draw on Google's local business data use category information to determine what a firm does and where it should appear in recommendation contexts. A firm categorised accurately as a "Family Law Attorney" is more likely to appear in Gemini's response to "I need a divorce lawyer in [city]" than a firm categorised only as "Law Firm." Category precision at the GBP level feeds directly into AI recommendation quality.

Combine correct GBP categories with strong content on your law firm website and comprehensive AI SEO signals, and your local visibility compounds across both traditional Google Maps and AI-powered local recommendations. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most important GBP category for a law firm?
Your primary category is the most important — it carries the most weight in local search rankings. For most law firms, 'Law Firm' is the correct primary category. Specialists may benefit from a more specific primary category: 'Personal Injury Attorney,' 'Criminal Justice Attorney,' 'Family Law Attorney,' etc. Avoid selecting a primary category that is too broad (like 'Business') or too narrow (like a specific court type).
How many secondary categories can a law firm add to GBP?
Google allows up to 10 categories total (1 primary + up to 9 secondary). You should use as many relevant secondary categories as accurately describe your practice areas. Common secondary categories for multi-practice law firms include: Legal Services, Attorney, Personal Injury Attorney, Criminal Justice Attorney, Family Law Attorney, Employment Attorney, Estate Planning Attorney, Real Estate Attorney, Immigration Attorney, Business Management Consultant.
Does changing GBP categories affect rankings?
Yes, significantly. Adding or changing categories can both improve and hurt rankings depending on the change. Adding highly relevant secondary categories typically improves visibility for those practice area searches. Removing a category you have ranked for can cause traffic drops. Always add carefully and avoid removing categories unless they are genuinely inaccurate — the algorithm takes time to adjust.
