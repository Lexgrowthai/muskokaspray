GBP for New Law Firms: Your First 90 Days Guide

Setting up Google Business Profile correctly from day one gives your new Ontario law firm the fastest possible path to local search visibility and Google Maps rankings. Here's exactly what to do in the first 90 days.

June 24, 2026
13 min read
LexScale.ai Editorial

Why GBP Is the Most Important Local Ranking Factor for New Ontario Law Firms

For a newly opened law firm in Ontario, the Google Business Profile is not one marketing channel among many — it is the single highest-leverage action you can take to generate local search visibility before your website has accumulated any domain authority, backlinks, or content depth. In the earliest months of practice, GBP is often the only place you will appear in search results for potential clients in your city.

The local pack — the three business listings that appear above organic results for queries like "personal injury lawyer Toronto" or "real estate lawyer Hamilton" — is driven almost entirely by GBP signals: category accuracy, profile completeness, review volume and recency, post activity, and proximity to the searcher. A well-optimized GBP can place a six-month-old firm above a competitor with a ten-year-old website but a neglected business profile.

46%
of all Google searches have local intent — and for legal services, that number is significantly higher as most people hire lawyers within their own city or region

For Ontario lawyers specifically, the local search opportunity is enormous. Potential clients in Brampton, Mississauga, Ottawa, Hamilton, and across the GTA search daily for lawyers in their specific city — they don't just search "family lawyer Ontario," they search "family lawyer Brampton near me." GBP is the mechanism Google uses to match those hyper-local queries to nearby businesses. Your GBP listing is your firm's presence in those results.

Beyond pure search ranking, GBP serves as a digital first impression. When a potential client sees your firm in the local pack, they see your star rating, review count, hours, phone number, and photos — all before visiting your website. A complete, well-maintained profile with strong reviews creates trust before the first contact. A sparse profile with no reviews and no photos signals uncertainty, even if your legal skills are excellent.

In 2026, the stakes are higher still. Google's AI Overviews now surface local law firm recommendations directly in search results for many legal queries. Gemini draws on GBP data — particularly reviews, category signals, and profile completeness — when generating these AI recommendations. A well-optimized GBP is now simultaneously a local search asset and an AI search asset. Getting it right from day one compounds in value every month you practice.

Day 1–7: Claiming and Verifying Your GBP Listing

DAYS 1–7

The first step is to check whether a GBP listing already exists for your firm. Google sometimes auto-creates listings based on data it finds from other sources — business registries, legal directories, or websites. Search for your firm name on Google Maps. If a listing exists, claim it rather than creating a duplicate. Duplicate listings dilute your authority and confuse Google's local ranking signals.

If no listing exists, go to business.google.com and click "Add your business." Enter your exact business name as it appears on your LSO registration and any public-facing signage. Consistency matters: if your firm is "Chen & Associates Professional Corporation," use that exact name everywhere — your website, GBP, Law Society directory, legal directories, and any other online presence. Name inconsistencies are a local SEO liability.

Address Requirements for Ontario Law Firms

Google requires a verifiable physical address. For Ontario law firms, your address must be a real office where you can receive mail — virtual office addresses at co-working spaces are permitted as long as you have a legitimate presence there. Your GBP address should match your Law Society of Ontario registration address. Any discrepancy between your LSO address and your GBP address sends mixed signals to both Google and clients researching your credentials.

If you are operating from a shared office arrangement, confirm that the building management permits you to use the address for business listings. Google occasionally audits GBP listings and may request re-verification if address legitimacy is questioned.

Verification Options

  • Postcard (most common): Google mails a postcard with a PIN to your business address. Arrives in 5-14 business days in Ontario. Enter the PIN in your GBP dashboard to complete verification.
  • Video verification: When offered, this is the fastest option — record a short video of your office exterior, signage, and interior showing it is an operating business. Verification typically completes within 24-72 hours.
  • Phone or email: Sometimes offered for new listings. Completes immediately with a code sent to your registered number or email.

Apply for verification on Day 1, then use the waiting period to complete the rest of your profile setup. Your listing will not appear in search results until verification completes, but all the setup work you do now will be live immediately after.

Day 8–14: Completing Your Profile

DAYS 8–14

Profile completeness is one of Google's most heavily weighted local ranking signals. Google has publicly stated that businesses with complete profiles are significantly more likely to be considered reputable and to appear in local search. Every field you leave blank is a missed signal. Your goal in this phase is 100% profile completion.

Category Selection

Your primary category is the most important single field in your GBP. It tells Google what type of business you are and determines which searches you are eligible to appear for. Select your primary category carefully:

  • General practice / multiple areas: Use "Law Firm" as your primary category
  • Personal injury focus: Use "Personal Injury Attorney" as primary
  • Family law focus: Use "Family Law Attorney" as primary
  • Criminal defence focus: Use "Criminal Justice Attorney" as primary
  • Real estate law focus: Use "Real Estate Attorney" as primary
  • Immigration focus: Use "Immigration & Naturalization Service" as primary

Add secondary categories for all additional practice areas your firm handles. You can add up to 9 additional categories. This allows you to appear for queries across multiple practice areas while maintaining a clear primary signal for your main focus area.

Business Description

Your GBP description can be up to 750 characters. Write it as you would a concise positioning statement — lead with your primary practice area, your geographic focus, and what differentiates your firm. Include 2-3 naturally occurring keyword phrases (your city + practice area, "Ontario law firm," etc.) without keyword stuffing. Avoid promotional language like "best" or "number one" — Google's guidelines discourage superlatives. Do mention: years of experience, specific practice areas, your geographic coverage, languages spoken if relevant, and any credentials or associations.

Hours, Phone, and Website

Set accurate hours and keep them updated — including holidays. A potential client calling during listed hours and reaching voicemail is a negative experience that may cost you a review. Add your direct phone number (not a call-tracking number that changes frequently — number consistency across platforms is a local SEO signal). Link to your website homepage. If you don't have a website yet, this is urgent — GBP without a website leaves significant authority and conversion opportunity on the table.

Services Section

GBP has a dedicated Services section where you can list your specific practice areas with descriptions. Use this section thoroughly. Each service entry can include a name and description. For example: "Personal Injury Claims — We represent Ontario clients in motor vehicle accident claims, slip-and-fall injuries, and catastrophic injury cases. No fees unless we win." This content is indexed by Google and contributes to your relevance for practice-area-specific queries.

Day 15–30: Your First Review Strategy

DAYS 15–30

Reviews are the most powerful ranking signal in local search. A new law firm with zero reviews is at a severe disadvantage against established competitors with dozens. Your first priority after completing your profile is building your initial review base — your first 10 reviews are the most important you'll ever get, because they establish your firm as an active, trusted business in Google's eyes.

For a new firm, your first reviews will likely come from:

  • Clients from any prior practice or employment (if they were your clients and the work was yours)
  • The first clients you serve after opening — prioritize follow-up with these clients immediately after matter resolution
  • Professional contacts who can speak to your expertise and character (note: LSO rules require that reviews represent honest client experiences — reviews from colleagues about your professional reputation fall into a grey area, so consult your understanding of the Rules of Professional Conduct)

The review request process for new firms should be personal and direct. Call satisfied clients within 48 hours of their matter resolving. Express your genuine appreciation for their trust. Ask directly: "If you're happy with how things went, a Google review would genuinely help other families in similar situations find our firm — it would mean a lot to us." Send the direct Google review link immediately by text or email. Your GBP review link is found in your dashboard under "Share profile."

LSO compliance reminder: You may ask satisfied former clients for honest reviews. You may not offer any incentive — financial or otherwise — for a review. Ask only after the matter is fully resolved and the client relationship has concluded. This is fully compliant under the Law Society of Ontario's Rules of Professional Conduct.

Respond to every review you receive — positive and negative. Responses demonstrate that your profile is actively managed (a positive local ranking signal) and create a public record of your client service values. For positive reviews, thank the reviewer by first name if used, acknowledge the matter type naturally, and express genuine appreciation. For negative reviews (they happen even to excellent lawyers), respond professionally, take the concern offline, and never reveal confidential case details.

Day 31–60: GBP Posts — What to Publish Weekly

DAYS 31–60

GBP Posts are one of the most underused features by law firms and one of the clearest signals to Google that your profile is actively managed. Posts appear directly in your GBP listing in search results, giving you additional real estate to communicate with potential clients before they visit your website. Post at minimum once per week — every 7 days keeps your profile flagged as active.

What to Post

Service spotlight posts: Each week, highlight one specific service your firm offers. "This week we're focusing on motor vehicle accident claims — if you or a family member was injured in a car accident in Ontario, our team handles the legal complexity so you can focus on recovery. Initial consultations are free." Include a link to the relevant page on your website or directly to your contact page.

Legal tips: Short, practical information your target clients can use. "Did you know Ontario's statute of limitations for most personal injury claims is 2 years from the date of the accident? Don't wait too long to get legal advice — contact us for a free consultation." These posts establish your expertise and are shared by clients who find them useful.

Office updates: New team member, extended hours, new practice area, office relocation — any legitimate business update. These signal that your firm is growing and active.

Event and community posts: Speaking at a local bar association event, sponsoring a community event, or attending a legal conference? Post about it. Community involvement signals local roots and builds trust with potential clients in your area.

Each post should include a call to action with a button — use "Book" for consultations, "Learn more" for educational content, or "Call now" for urgent inquiries. Include a high-quality photo where possible; posts with photos receive significantly higher engagement than text-only posts. Day 61–90: Q&A Optimization, Photos, and Tracking Insights

DAYS 61–90

Q&A Section

The GBP Q&A section allows anyone — including you — to submit questions and answers. Many law firms don't realize you can proactively seed your own Q&A section with the questions your potential clients most commonly ask. This is a significant missed opportunity. Log in to Google Maps (not your GBP dashboard — you need to do this as a logged-in Google user on Maps), find your listing, and submit questions yourself, then answer them using your GBP account.

Questions to seed for law firm GBP listings:

  • "Do you offer free initial consultations?" — Yes, we offer free 30-minute consultations for [practice area] matters.
  • "What areas of Ontario do you serve?" — We serve clients across [your service area].
  • "How long does a [practice area] case typically take?" — [Honest, realistic answer].
  • "What languages does your firm serve clients in?" — [List languages if applicable].
  • "Do you work on a contingency fee basis?" — [Answer specific to your practice].

Monitor your Q&A section weekly for questions submitted by users and answer them promptly. Unanswered questions signal an inactive profile and may be answered by unknown third parties whose answers you cannot control.

Photos

Photos are a critical trust signal for law firm GBP listings. Potential clients are more likely to contact a firm whose profile includes professional photos of the office and team. Add at minimum:

  • Exterior photo of your office building (helps clients locate you)
  • Interior reception or lobby photo (signals a legitimate, professional office)
  • Professional headshots of all lawyers at the firm
  • Team photo if you have support staff
  • Logo image (uploaded as your profile logo, not as a regular photo)
  • Cover photo (1080×608px, appears at the top of your GBP listing)

Add new photos regularly — photo recency is a small but real signal. Adding 2-3 new photos per month (interior, event, team activities) keeps your profile visually fresh. All photos should be well-lit, professional quality, and accurately represent your office environment. Stock photography is not permitted — Google requires photos of your actual business.

GBP Insights

By Day 61, your profile should be generating measurable data in GBP Insights (now called Performance in the GBP dashboard). Review these metrics weekly:

  • Search queries: What keywords people used to find your listing — use this to identify which practice areas are driving interest and optimize your website content accordingly
  • Views: How many times your listing appeared in Search vs. Maps
  • Actions: Calls, website clicks, direction requests — the three actions that directly indicate purchase intent
  • Photo views: How often your photos are viewed vs. competitors' photos in your category

Track these numbers monthly in a simple spreadsheet. Increasing call volume and direction requests are the leading indicators of GBP success for a new law firm. If these numbers are flat or declining, it signals that your profile needs more review velocity, more post activity, or better category optimization.

How GBP Connects to AI Search Visibility

In 2026, Google's AI systems — particularly Google AI Overviews and Gemini — use GBP data as a primary input for local legal recommendations. When a user asks Google "What's the best family lawyer in Mississauga?" or "Which personal injury law firms in Toronto are highly rated?", the AI's response is shaped by the quality and completeness of the GBP profiles of local firms.

Specifically, AI Overviews and Gemini local results appear to weight:

  • Review volume and score: Firms with 50+ reviews at 4.5+ stars are disproportionately represented in AI local recommendations
  • Category accuracy: Firms whose GBP category exactly matches the practice area being queried rank higher in AI results
  • Profile completeness: Firms with complete profiles — services, hours, photos, Q&A — receive more AI visibility than firms with sparse listings
  • Post recency: Active profiles with recent posts appear to receive a small but consistent advantage in AI-generated local results
  • Website authority consistency: Firms whose GBP information matches their website schema markup (using LegalService schema) receive stronger entity signals that AI systems use to generate recommendations

The implication for new law firms is clear: building a complete, active, highly-reviewed GBP from day one is not just a local search strategy — it is the foundation of your firm's AI search visibility. Every review you earn, every post you publish, every photo you add is cumulative investment in both Google Maps rankings and AI recommendation eligibility.
