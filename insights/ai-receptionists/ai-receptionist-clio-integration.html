AI Receptionist Clio Integration: Automate Your Intake Pipeline

Connect your AI receptionist to Clio Manage to automatically create contacts, matters, and intake notes from every call — without touching your keyboard.

June 24, 2026 12 min read LexScale.ai Editorial

Why Clio Is the Dominant Canadian Law Firm Platform

Clio was founded in Vancouver in 2008 and has grown to become the most widely used cloud-based practice management platform in North America, with particular penetration in Canada. More than 60% of Canadian law firms that use practice management software run on Clio Manage — a dominance that makes Clio integration not a nice-to-have feature for Canadian legal tech tools, but a baseline requirement. If your AI receptionist cannot connect to Clio, you have created a manual data entry bottleneck that negates a significant portion of the efficiency gains the AI receptionist is supposed to provide.

Clio Manage is the core case management module — it stores contacts, matters, documents, time entries, billing, and firm calendar. Clio Grow is the intake and CRM module, designed specifically for managing the pre-engagement pipeline: lead capture, intake questionnaires, consultation scheduling, and engagement letter generation. For the AI receptionist integration, Clio Manage is the primary target (contact and matter creation), though firms using Clio Grow can also push intake data there to leverage its pipeline management features.

The reason Clio integration matters so much is the elimination of the re-entry problem. Without integration, a completed AI receptionist intake call generates a summary that arrives in your email or dashboard. A staff member then reads that summary and manually creates the Clio contact and matter. This takes 3–8 minutes per call. For a firm receiving 20 new inquiry calls per day, that is 60–160 minutes of manual data entry daily — work that adds no value and introduces transcription errors. With a proper Clio integration, this entire process is eliminated: the contact and matter are in Clio before the call ends.

Clio reports that law firms using their platform save an average of 8 hours per week on administrative tasks. Adding AI receptionist integration to automate intake data entry compounds this saving — eliminating the manual bridge between call handling and matter creation that remains the largest remaining administrative gap in most legal intake workflows.

What the AI Receptionist to Clio Integration Actually Does

The AI receptionist to Clio integration is not simply a "send email to Clio" feature. A properly configured integration creates a structured data flow that mirrors what a skilled paralegal would do after listening to an intake call — but instantly and without manual steps. Understanding what the integration actually does at each stage clarifies why it matters and what you need to configure correctly.

Auto-create contacts. When a caller completes an intake call, the AI receptionist extracts structured contact data — first name, last name, phone number, email address, and physical address if collected — and creates a new contact record in Clio Manage. If the caller already exists in Clio (matching on phone number or email), the integration updates the existing record rather than creating a duplicate. This deduplication logic is critical for firms with returning prospects or existing clients calling from multiple numbers.

Auto-create matters. Alongside the contact, the integration creates a new matter record in Clio linked to the contact. The matter description is populated from the intake summary — typically a structured paragraph describing the nature of the legal issue, the adverse party (if identified), the relevant dates, and the urgency level as assessed by the AI. The matter's practice area is mapped from the intake category (e.g., "Personal Injury" maps to your PI practice area in Clio).

Populate intake notes. The full call transcript — or a structured intake note derived from it — is added to the Clio matter as a note. This gives the attorney reviewing the matter a complete record of what was discussed on the intake call without listening to the recording. The note typically includes: caller's description of their situation in their own words, key dates mentioned, prior legal representation status, conflict-of-interest information (adverse party name), and the caller's preferred contact method and availability.

Set matter status and assignments. The integration can set the initial matter status (typically "Intake" or "Pending Review") and assign the matter to a specific attorney or intake coordinator based on rules you configure — for example, all criminal defence matters go to attorney A, all family law matters go to attorney B. This routing logic eliminates the need for a human dispatcher to review each intake and assign it manually.

Native vs. Zapier Integration: Which Services Support What

There are two types of Clio integration for AI receptionist services: native integrations (built directly into the AI receptionist platform using Clio's official API) and indirect integrations (using Zapier or similar automation middleware to bridge the two platforms). Native integrations are faster, more reliable, and support deeper field mapping. Zapier integrations are more flexible and available for services that have not built native Clio connections.

Step-by-Step: Connecting Smith.ai to Clio Manage

Smith.ai's native Clio integration is the most complete available and requires no third-party tools. The following steps assume you have an active Smith.ai account (any paid plan) and a Clio Manage account with administrator access.

1

Authorize Smith.ai in Clio's App Directory

Log into Clio Manage as an administrator. Navigate to Settings → Integrations → App Directory. Search for "Smith.ai" and click Connect. You will be redirected to Smith.ai's OAuth authorization page. Sign in with your Smith.ai credentials and click "Allow Access." This grants Smith.ai permission to create and edit contacts, matters, and notes in your Clio account. The authorization token is stored securely by Smith.ai and does not require renewal unless you revoke it.

2

Configure Field Mapping in Your Smith.ai Dashboard

Inside your Smith.ai account, go to Integrations → Clio. You will see a field mapping interface that shows Smith.ai intake fields on the left and Clio contact/matter fields on the right. Map caller first name to Clio Contact: First Name; caller last name to Clio Contact: Last Name; caller phone to Clio Contact: Phone; caller email to Clio Contact: Email; and intake summary to Clio Matter: Description. If you have custom Clio fields (e.g., a "Referred By" field or an "Incident Date" field), map the corresponding Smith.ai intake question to those custom fields here.

3

Configure Practice Area Routing Rules

Smith.ai allows you to define routing rules based on intake call category. In your Smith.ai dashboard, go to Intake Settings → Practice Areas. Create a practice area entry for each area your firm handles (e.g., Personal Injury, Family Law, Criminal Defence, Real Estate). For each practice area, specify the corresponding Clio practice area category and the default attorney or staff member to assign the matter to. This routing logic runs automatically on every completed intake call — no manual assignment needed.

4

Enable Transcript-to-Note Syncing

In your Smith.ai integration settings, enable "Sync call summary to Clio Matter Notes." Choose whether to sync the AI-generated intake summary (structured, concise) or the full call transcript (complete but longer). For most law firms, the AI-generated summary is more useful for attorney review — it highlights key facts without requiring the attorney to read a full conversation transcript. Enable the "Flag urgent matters" option so that calls the AI identifies as time-sensitive create a Clio note with a priority flag.

5

Run a Test Call and Verify the Clio Record

Call your Smith.ai number from a personal phone and complete a full intake as if you were a new client. Use a test name (e.g., "Test Client Integration") and fictional case details. After the call ends, wait 60–90 seconds, then check your Clio Manage account. You should see a new contact created with your test details, a linked matter with the intake summary in the description, and a note containing the call summary. Verify that all field mappings are correct and that the practice area routing assigned the matter to the right attorney. If anything is missing or incorrect, revisit the field mapping and routing configuration before going live.

Setting Up Zapier Connections for Services Without Native Integration

For AI receptionist services that do not have native Clio integration — such as Ruby Receptionists — Zapier provides a reliable bridge. Zapier is a no-code automation platform that connects web apps via "Zaps" (automated workflows). The core Zapier workflow for AI receptionist to Clio integration is: Trigger (new message/intake from your AI receptionist) → Action 1 (create Clio contact) → Action 2 (create Clio matter linked to that contact) → Action 3 (add note to the matter with the intake summary).

To build this Zapier workflow for Ruby Receptionists: log into Zapier and click "Create Zap." Set the trigger app to Ruby Receptionists and choose "New Message" as the trigger event. Connect your Ruby account when prompted. For the first action, set the app to Clio and the event to "Create Contact." Map the Ruby message fields to Clio contact fields — Ruby provides structured message data with caller name, phone, and message body. For the second action, add another Clio step: "Create Matter." Use the contact ID output from step one to link the matter to the contact you just created. For the third action, add "Create Note in Clio" and map the Ruby message body to the note content. Set the note's related resource to the matter created in step two.

Zapier plan considerations: The free Zapier plan supports only single-step Zaps. A multi-step Zap (trigger + 3 actions) requires a paid Zapier plan, starting at approximately USD $19.99/month for the Starter tier. This is a necessary additional cost for Ruby-to-Clio integration that should be budgeted alongside your Ruby subscription. The Starter plan also limits you to 750 Zap runs per month — sufficient for most small and mid-size firms but potentially restrictive for high-volume intake operations.

Zapier vs. native integration reliability: In practice, well-configured Zapier integrations are highly reliable (99%+ uptime). The main risk is authentication failures when either the Ruby or Clio API token expires or is revoked. Set up Zapier error notifications (email alert when a Zap fails) so you are immediately alerted to any integration breakdowns. For mission-critical intake, consider Smith.ai's native integration as it eliminates this dependency entirely.

PIPEDA Considerations When Syncing Call Data to Clio

For Canadian law firms — particularly those in Ontario and Quebec — the integration of AI receptionist call data into Clio raises several PIPEDA compliance considerations that are worth addressing before going live. PIPEDA governs how personal information is collected, used, disclosed, and retained, and the AI receptionist to Clio data pipeline involves all of these activities.

Consent at collection. The AI receptionist must inform callers that their call is being recorded and that the information they provide will be stored in your firm's case management system. This disclosure should occur at the beginning of every intake call — typically as part of the AI's opening greeting: "I'd like to let you know this call may be recorded for quality and intake purposes. The information you provide will be kept confidential and stored securely." This satisfies PIPEDA's consent requirement at the point of collection.

Data minimization. Only the information necessary for the legal matter should be synced to Clio. Avoid configuring your integration to push raw full-length call recordings to Clio notes — these contain more personal information than needed and increase storage obligations. Structured intake summaries (name, contact details, matter description, adverse party, key dates) are sufficient for Clio records and minimize the personal information footprint.

Cross-border transfer documentation. If your AI receptionist service (e.g., Smith.ai, Ruby) stores call data on US servers before syncing it to Clio, this constitutes a cross-border transfer of personal information under PIPEDA. You must have a written contract with the service provider that obligates them to provide equivalent privacy protection to what PIPEDA requires. Request and review the service provider's Data Processing Agreement (DPA) before deploying. Clio itself stores Canadian law firm data in Canada (AWS Canada Central, ca-central-1 region), so data in Clio is Canadian-resident — only the upstream receptionist service creates the cross-border exposure.

Retention and deletion. Configure your AI receptionist service to automatically delete call recordings after a specified retention period (typically 90 days — long enough for intake disputes but not indefinite). The structured intake data in Clio should follow your firm's standard matter data retention policy. When a matter is closed and the retention period has expired, ensure that both the Clio record and any residual data in the AI receptionist platform are deleted in accordance with PIPEDA's accuracy and retention principles.

Testing the Integration: A 5-Step Verification Checklist

Before going live with your AI receptionist Clio integration, run through this five-step verification checklist. Each step tests a specific component of the integration and identifies any configuration errors before they affect real client data.

1

New Contact Creation Test

Call your AI receptionist number and complete a full intake using a fictional name and unique phone number not already in Clio. After the call, check Clio Contacts for the new entry. Verify: first name, last name, phone number, and email are all present and accurately transcribed. If any field is missing or wrong, check the field mapping in your integration settings.

2

Matter Creation and Linkage Test

In Clio Manage, navigate to Matters and search for the test matter created from your test call. Verify the matter is linked to the correct contact, the practice area is assigned correctly, the matter description contains a meaningful summary of your test call scenario, and the matter status is set to your configured intake status (e.g., "Pending"). Check that the responsible attorney assignment matched your routing rules.

3

Note Content and Accuracy Test

Open the test matter in Clio and navigate to its Notes. Verify a note was created containing the intake summary or transcript. Confirm that names, dates, and legal issue descriptions mentioned during your test call are accurately captured in the note. If your AI receptionist generates both a summary and a transcript, confirm which one is being synced to Clio (most firms prefer the structured summary).

4

Deduplication Test

Call your AI receptionist again using the same phone number as the test call in step 1. After the call, check Clio to verify a second contact was not created — the new matter should link to the existing contact from the first test call. If a duplicate contact was created, your integration lacks deduplication logic; contact your AI receptionist service's support team to enable phone-number-based deduplication.

5

Failure Notification Test

Temporarily revoke the Clio integration authorization in your AI receptionist dashboard (or disconnect the Zapier Zap), then make a test intake call. Verify that you receive an alert — email notification, dashboard warning, or SMS — informing you that the Clio sync failed for that call, and that the intake data is still accessible in the AI receptionist platform. Restore the authorization after the test. This confirms your failure fallback is working before it matters in production.

Once all five steps pass without errors, your integration is ready for live deployment. Schedule a brief training session with your intake team and attorneys to walk them through what they will see in Clio after each AI receptionist call — the contact record, the linked matter, and the intake note — so they know exactly where to look and what to expect.
