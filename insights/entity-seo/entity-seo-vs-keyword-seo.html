Entity SEO vs Keyword SEO for Law Firms

SEO has evolved from keyword matching to entity authority. This complete comparison explains how both approaches work, where they overlap, and how to build a strategy that dominates both channels.

June 19, 2025 18 min read LexScale.ai Editorial

Two Eras of SEO: From Keywords to Entities

Search engine optimization has evolved through two distinct eras that reflect the fundamental evolution of how search engines understand the web. The first era — which dominated SEO practice from the late 1990s through the mid-2010s — was defined by keyword optimization: the practice of strategically using specific words and phrases on web pages to match the text-based pattern-matching algorithms of early search engines. The second era — which is defining SEO practice today and into the foreseeable future — is defined by entity optimization: the practice of establishing and enhancing the machine-readable identity of real-world people, places, and organizations in AI systems and knowledge graphs.

Understanding the transition from keyword SEO to entity SEO is important for law firms because it explains why some traditional SEO tactics are becoming less effective and why new tactics are increasingly necessary. It also explains the underlying mechanism through which AI search tools decide which law firms to recommend — a mechanism that is far less about keyword matching and far more about entity authority and identity.

This article provides a comprehensive comparison of entity SEO and keyword SEO for law firms: what each approach optimizes for, how they work mechanically, what tactics each requires, and how to build a strategy that leverages the strengths of both approaches in a unified investment that serves your firm across all search channels.

The law firms that will dominate search — both traditional and AI — over the next decade are the ones that invest in both keyword optimization for their page content AND entity authority for their firm identity. Neither alone is sufficient.

How Keyword SEO Works: The Mechanics

Keyword SEO operates at the page level. The fundamental assumption is that search engines match queries to pages based on the textual overlap between the query and the page content. To rank for 'personal injury lawyer Chicago,' you need a page that contains those words in important positions (title tag, H1, URL, first paragraph), has sufficient inbound links to demonstrate that other websites consider the page authoritative, and loads quickly enough and is structured clearly enough to provide a good user experience.

The tactics that keyword SEO requires are well-established: keyword research to identify what terms your prospective clients search, on-page optimization to strategically place those terms in the right positions and densities, link building to earn inbound links from authoritative websites, technical SEO to ensure fast loading and clean site structure, and content creation to cover the full range of relevant topics. These tactics have been refined over more than two decades of practice and remain largely effective for traditional Google search.

Keyword SEO is measurable and actionable in ways that feel familiar and concrete: you can see your keyword rankings, track changes over time, identify pages that need more links or better optimization, and directly connect specific keyword positions to organic traffic and leads. This measurability makes keyword SEO comfortable for firms that need clear data to justify investment. The limitation is that keyword SEO does not address the increasingly important question of how AI systems understand and represent your firm as an entity — which is where entity SEO fills the gap.

How Entity SEO Works: The Mechanics

Entity SEO operates at the identity level. The fundamental assumption is that AI systems and modern search engines maintain knowledge graphs — databases of real-world entities and their attributes — and that search results and AI responses are generated by querying these knowledge graphs, not just by matching text. To be recommended by an AI system for 'personal injury lawyer Chicago,' your firm needs to be a well-defined entity in the relevant knowledge graphs with accurate practice area, location, and reputation attributes.

The tactics that entity SEO requires are different from keyword SEO: structured data markup (schema.org) to explicitly label your entity's attributes on your website, NAP consistency across all directories to create a clear, unambiguous entity record, professional profile completeness on authoritative platforms (state bar, Avvo, Martindale, LinkedIn), third-party validation through media mentions and publications, and review signal building to establish reputation data. These tactics build entity authority rather than keyword relevance.

Entity SEO is less immediately measurable than keyword SEO, which makes it feel less concrete despite its growing importance. You cannot easily track your 'entity ranking' the way you track keyword rankings. Instead, you track proxy metrics: Knowledge Panel presence, AI citation frequency, NAP consistency score across directories, directory profile completeness scores, and review aggregate data. These metrics require different tools and a longer time horizon than keyword tracking, but they measure dimensions of search authority that keyword metrics miss entirely.

Where Keyword and Entity SEO Overlap

Despite their different approaches, keyword SEO and entity SEO overlap significantly in their underlying infrastructure requirements. High-quality, comprehensive content satisfies both: it provides keyword relevance signals for traditional search and demonstrates entity expertise that AI systems value. Fast-loading, technically clean websites serve both: they rank better in traditional search and are more accessible to AI crawlers. Domain authority from inbound links benefits both: it signals authority in traditional search algorithms and contributes to entity credibility signals.

This substantial overlap means that investing in high-quality SEO serves both channels simultaneously. The mistake to avoid is believing that optimizing for keywords alone is sufficient for AI search visibility, or that entity SEO alone eliminates the need for keyword optimization. The optimal strategy is integrated — designing content, technical infrastructure, and authority-building activities to serve both channels simultaneously.

The content format that most naturally serves both channels is comprehensive, well-structured articles that combine keyword targeting with entity-relevant expertise signals. A 2,500-word guide to car accident claims in Texas that targets the keyword 'Texas car accident lawyer' while also demonstrating deep topical expertise, citing relevant law, and attributing content to a named, credentialed attorney is performing keyword SEO and entity SEO simultaneously. This is the content format law firms should be producing consistently.

Keyword SEO Tactics That Still Work in the Entity Era

Despite the shift toward entity thinking, many classic keyword SEO tactics remain highly effective. On-page optimization — using target keywords in title tags, H1s, URLs, and early paragraph text — still influences traditional Google rankings significantly. Long-tail keyword targeting — creating specific pages for specific search phrases like 'car accident settlement timeline Texas' or 'how to file a workers compensation claim in Florida' — remains effective for capturing highly specific queries that AI tools are less likely to fully synthesize.

Local keyword optimization — including city names, county names, and regional terminology in your content — still matters for local search results and supports the geographic context that local AI recommendations depend on. Meta description optimization — writing compelling meta descriptions that improve click-through rates — remains important because click signals influence both traditional rankings and the kind of user engagement metrics that AI systems use to evaluate content quality.

Technical keyword SEO — ensuring proper site structure, canonical URLs, and crawlable page architecture — remains a prerequisite for both traditional and AI search. Without technical SEO health, both keyword optimization and entity SEO are limited in their effectiveness. Think of technical SEO as the foundation that both tracks require — you cannot do effective keyword SEO or entity SEO on a technically broken website.

Entity SEO Tactics That Keyword SEO Cannot Provide

Entity SEO addresses capabilities and benefits that keyword SEO simply cannot provide, regardless of how well it is executed. The clearest example is AI citation in platforms where your page has not been indexed in real time. When a user asks ChatGPT about lawyers in your city, ChatGPT draws on its training data — which was collected months or years ago — and on its knowledge graph understanding of entities in that domain. A law firm with strong entity authority in that training data and knowledge graph is recommended; a firm visible only in recent keyword-optimized pages may not be.

Knowledge Panel presence is another entity SEO outcome that keyword SEO cannot generate. No amount of keyword optimization produces an attorney Knowledge Panel — that requires entity authority signals from authoritative third-party sources. Similarly, AI Overview citation frequency — how often your firm is cited in Google's AI-generated summaries — is driven more by entity authority and content comprehensiveness than by keyword optimization specifics.

Reputation signals — the sentiment, volume, and distribution of reviews across platforms — are entity SEO factors with no keyword SEO analog. Reviews are not on-page content to optimize — they are third-party endorsements that feed into your entity's reputation attributes in knowledge graphs. Building your review profile is a purely entity SEO activity, and it is one of the highest-impact activities for local AI recommendation visibility.

Building an Integrated Strategy for Both Approaches

The most effective SEO strategy for law firms in 2025 is an integrated approach that pursues keyword optimization and entity authority building simultaneously, with a unified investment that serves both channels. Here is how to structure that integrated strategy.

Start with the shared foundation: technical SEO health, quality content, and domain authority. These investments serve both channels and should be the highest priority for any firm starting or upgrading its digital strategy. A technically clean, fast-loading website with high-quality, comprehensive content and a growing inbound link profile is the prerequisite for success at both keyword SEO and entity SEO.

Then add channel-specific tactical layers. For keyword SEO: keyword research, on-page optimization, meta description optimization, local keyword targeting, and technical structured URL architecture. For entity SEO: schema markup across all page types, NAP audit and correction, directory profile completion, review acquisition systematization, attorney bio page optimization with schema, and media outreach for third-party mentions. These channel-specific tactics can be pursued in parallel without conflict — most require different team members or vendors and can run simultaneously.

Prioritizing Investment Based on Your Current Position

How you prioritize between keyword and entity SEO investment depends on your current position. Law firms that have neglected keyword SEO basics — thin content, poor technical health, no link building, low organic traffic — should prioritize keyword SEO fundamentals first to build the traffic base that makes entity SEO pay off. Entity SEO generates AI citation recommendations, but those recommendations ultimately drive website visits and consultations — you need a functional, credible website to convert that traffic.

Law firms that already have solid keyword SEO performance — reasonable organic rankings, meaningful traffic, functional content — should prioritize entity SEO investments that their keyword-focused competitors are likely neglecting. Schema markup, directory profile completion, attorney Knowledge Panel development, and review systematization are areas where many law firms with strong keyword SEO have significant gaps. Filling those gaps creates a competitive advantage in AI search visibility that compounds over time.

The most sophisticated law firms pursue both simultaneously through a unified strategy that assigns different team members or vendors to each track: an SEO specialist focused on keyword research, content optimization, and link building; and a digital marketing manager focused on schema implementation, directory management, review acquisition, and entity authority building. Together, these tracks produce a comprehensive digital presence that dominates both traditional and AI search.
