How Google Gemini Handles Local Search for Lawyers

Asking Gemini for a local lawyer recommendation produces very different results than a Google search. Understanding how it works — and what it favours — is how you get ahead of competitors.

11 min read
Published June 23, 2026
LexScale.ai Editorial

How Local Search Works Inside Google Gemini

When someone opens Gemini and types "find me a real estate lawyer in Hamilton, Ontario," something more sophisticated than a directory lookup happens. Gemini synthesizes information from multiple sources simultaneously — Google Business Profile data, local directory listings including Justia, Avvo, and the Canadian Legal Information Institute, website content, and review signals — and produces a recommendation that is intended to feel like advice from a knowledgeable friend who knows the local legal market.

This is the key distinction that Ontario law firms need to internalize. Gemini does not simply surface the nearest law firm with the highest star rating, the way Google Maps does. It attempts to match the specific legal need expressed in the query to the firm that is best positioned to address it. A query about real estate law in Hamilton will surface different results than a query about family law in Hamilton — even if the same firm does both — because Gemini weights the content signals that correspond to the specific practice area being asked about.

Understanding this mechanism is the foundation of everything that follows. If you want to appear in Gemini's local recommendations for "family lawyer in Mississauga," your firm's signals around family law in Mississauga need to be strong. Generic signals about being a law firm in the GTA are not enough. The specificity of the match matters.

The Difference Between Gemini Local Results and Google Maps

Google Maps and Google Gemini both draw on Google Business Profile data, but they use it very differently. Understanding the distinction helps clarify what optimization work actually matters for each platform — and why firms that are strong on Maps may not automatically appear in Gemini's recommendations, and vice versa.

Google Maps ranks businesses primarily on proximity to the searcher and overall rating, supplemented by review count and GBP completeness. If you search for "criminal defence lawyer Brampton" on Maps, you will get results ranked substantially by how close the firm is to your location and how many stars it has. Content depth is a marginal factor on Maps. Distance and rating dominate.

Gemini goes further. A query like "I need a criminal defence lawyer who handles impaired driving charges in Brampton" will surface results from Gemini that reflect a richer analysis — including whether the firm's website has substantive content about impaired driving defence in Ontario, whether the firm's reviews mention impaired driving cases specifically, and whether the firm is recognized in directories and other sources as having expertise in that area.

That same firm may not appear in Gemini's recommendations for queries where content depth matters — and for legal queries, content depth almost always matters, because people asking Gemini about lawyers are almost always expressing a specific need, not just looking for the nearest office.

What Signals Gemini Uses to Choose Local Lawyers

Gemini's local recommendations emerge from a combination of signals that collectively establish a picture of a firm's authority, relevance, and reputation in a specific geographic and practice area context. No single signal dominates — but some matter more than others for different types of queries.

01
Google Business Profile Completeness
A fully completed GBP — all categories, service areas, hours, description, photos, and regularly updated posts — is the foundation. GBP data is one of Gemini's primary inputs for local queries. Firms with incomplete GBPs are invisible to Gemini's local layer.
02
Review Volume and Recency
Recent reviews outweigh old ones in Gemini's weighting. A firm with 15 reviews in the past six months will generally signal stronger authority than a firm with 80 reviews spread across five years. Consistent review acquisition matters more than historical totals.
03
Review Content and Keywords
Gemini reads review text, not just star ratings. Reviews that mention specific practice areas ("helped us with our separation agreement," "handled my impaired driving charge") tell Gemini what type of law the firm actually does well. This is how practice-area matching happens in review signals.
04
Website Content Depth
Practice area pages that go beyond surface-level descriptions — explaining how family law works in Ontario, what to expect in a Brampton family court proceeding, how parenting time disputes are resolved — signal genuine expertise. Thin pages do not.
05
Directory Consistency (NAP)
Name, address, and phone number consistency across Justia, FindLaw, the LSO lawyer directory, CanLII citations, and local business directories strengthens the entity profile Gemini uses to identify and verify your firm. Inconsistencies create confusion and weaken authority signals.
06
Ontario-Specific Backlinks
Links from Ontario-specific legal and local sources — community organizations, local news coverage, regional bar association mentions, Ontario legal aid referral sources — are stronger local signals than generic legal directory backlinks. Geographic specificity of link sources matters.

The signal that surprises most law firms is review content. Most firms focus on getting five-star ratings and assume the text doesn't matter. For Gemini, the text is the data. A five-star review that says "great lawyer, highly recommend" tells Gemini almost nothing. A review that says "helped us navigate a complicated custody dispute involving Brampton Family Court — understood the nuances of Ontario parenting time law and got us a fair outcome" is a powerful signal that this firm handles custody disputes in Brampton. Multiply that across dozens of reviews and you have a strong local signal for that specific practice area in that specific city. How to Structure Your Online Presence for Local Gemini Recommendations

The structural work required to optimize for Gemini local recommendations is an extension of good local SEO — but with a higher content bar and a more explicit focus on the signals Gemini actually reads. Here is what the work looks like in practice.

Create city-specific practice area landing pages. This is the single highest-leverage structural change most Ontario law firms can make. A page like "/family-lawyer-mississauga.html" or "/criminal-defence-lawyer-brampton.html" does something a generic "practice areas" page cannot: it explicitly tells Gemini that this firm handles this type of law in this specific city. These pages need to contain substantive content — not just a city name inserted into a generic template, but actual information about practising in that city, the relevant courts, local nuances, and what clients in that area typically face.

Update your GBP service area listing. Your Google Business Profile should list every city and region you legitimately serve. A Toronto family law firm that also serves Mississauga, Brampton, and Vaughan should have all of those listed. GBP service areas are one of the primary ways Gemini determines geographic reach. Firms that only list their office city miss recommendations for the surrounding areas they actually serve.

Get listed on Ontario-specific directories. The Law Society of Ontario's lawyer directory is the most authoritative Ontario-specific listing for any firm. Beyond that, key directories include the Law Society Referral Service, community legal clinic directories for firms doing legal aid work, and regional bar association member directories. For immigration firms, the Canadian Bar Association's immigration section directory matters. These Ontario-specific listings carry more geographic weight than generic international directories.

Implement Attorney and LocalBusiness schema markup. Structured data tells Gemini explicitly what you are, where you are, and what you do. An Attorney schema on your practice area pages that includes the city, practice area, and geographic service area is a direct signal to Gemini's entity recognition layer. This is particularly valuable for firms serving multiple cities, because schema can explicitly declare each service area without requiring a separate page for every permutation. Our guide on Gemini and Google Business Profile for law firms covers the technical setup in detail.

Build local content that demonstrates market knowledge. Content about Ontario courts, Ontario-specific legal procedures, changes to Ontario family law regulations, and local legal aid resources signals geographic expertise in a way that entity optimization alone cannot. A page that explains how the Ottawa courthouse handles property division in divorce proceedings tells Gemini something fundamentally different about your firm's Ontario expertise than a page that just says "we serve Ottawa." City-Specific Content Strategies That Work for Gemini

There is a meaningful difference between inserting a city name into generic legal content and actually writing content that reflects local legal market knowledge. Gemini can tell the difference — not because it is infallible, but because genuinely local content contains specifics that generic content cannot replicate.

Consider what meaningful city-specific content looks like for a few Ontario markets. A Kitchener-Waterloo immigration lawyer's content should reflect the realities of practising immigration law in that market: the proximity of the Waterloo Regional Immigration Partnership, the immigration patterns specific to the tech corridor (Labour Market Impact Assessments for the startup sector, international student pathways at Waterloo and Wilfrid Laurier), and the regional settlement services that assist new arrivals. Content that references these specifics could only have been written by someone who actually knows the Kitchener-Waterloo immigration law market.

A Windsor real estate lawyer serving a market defined by cross-border proximity to Detroit faces questions that no Toronto real estate lawyer typically handles: dual-currency property issues, clients considering U.S. property purchases alongside Ontario acquisitions, Windsor-Essex County specific zoning considerations in border-adjacent areas, and the regional real estate board's market patterns. Content that addresses these specifics signals genuine Windsor market expertise. Content that just says "real estate lawyer in Windsor" signals nothing.

For London, Ontario, a personal injury firm might focus on content about the Middlesex County Court of Justice, the specific insurance claim patterns in Southwestern Ontario, and the role of London Health Sciences Centre as a key institution in catastrophic injury cases in that region. For a Hamilton family law firm, content about the Hamilton courthouse scheduling, the Unified Family Court in Hamilton, and the specific demographic patterns affecting family law in the steel city would all constitute genuine local knowledge.

The Local Knowledge Test
Apply this test to any piece of local content before publishing: does this content contain specific information that only someone who actually practises in this market would know? If a lawyer from Ottawa could have written the same content about their Ottawa practice with just the city name changed, the content fails the test. If the content references local courts, regional patterns, area-specific legal issues, or community knowledge that is genuinely tied to the city in question, it passes. Gemini rewards the latter. The former is noise.

Ottawa deserves its own note because it is a bilingual legal market with distinct characteristics. A French-language family law firm in Ottawa's Francophone community faces a different content opportunity than an English-language firm across the city. Content about Ontario's Francophone legal aid services, the Cour de l'Ontario's French-language proceedings, and the specific family law resources available through Francophone community organizations in Ottawa would constitute highly specific, genuinely local expertise that Gemini would weight as authoritative for queries from French-speaking Ottawans seeking family law assistance.

The same logic applies across the province: Toronto, Mississauga, Ottawa, Hamilton, Brampton, Kitchener-Waterloo, London, Windsor — each has specific legal market characteristics that, if reflected accurately in your content, make your firm's digital presence more credible to Gemini as a local authority. This is not about volume of content; it is about specificity and accuracy. Two or three pages of genuinely local content outperform ten pages of city-stuffed generics for Gemini visibility purposes.

Tracking Your Local Gemini Visibility

One of the honest challenges of Gemini local optimization is that the measurement tools are less mature than those available for traditional local SEO. Google has not yet provided direct analytics for Gemini recommendation appearances the way it provides data for Maps and Search Console. That means tracking local Gemini visibility currently requires a combination of proxy metrics and manual testing.

Manual testing. The most direct way to assess your Gemini local visibility is to run the searches yourself. Open the Gemini app or use Google Search's AI features and search for your core service queries in your cities: "family lawyer in [city]," "criminal defence lawyer [city] Ontario," "[practice area] lawyer [city]." Note whether your firm appears, where it appears, and what the recommendation says about it. Do this for the cities you serve, not just your office location. Test both specific and general queries — "best family lawyer Mississauga" and "divorce lawyer for separation with children Mississauga" will sometimes surface different results.

GBP Insights as a proxy. Google Business Profile Insights tracks profile views, phone calls, website clicks, and direction requests originating from your profile. While this data doesn't distinguish between Maps and Gemini traffic, increases in GBP-sourced phone calls and website visits from your target cities correlate with improved local AI visibility. Track these monthly and look for sustained upward trends following optimization work.

Traditional local keyword rankings. Rankings for city-specific practice area keywords in traditional Google Search correlate meaningfully with Gemini local visibility. The content and entity signals that move you up in traditional local rankings are substantially the same signals that improve Gemini recommendations. Track rank positions for "[practice area] lawyer [city]" queries in your target markets using any standard rank tracking tool. The correlation isn't perfect, but improvement in traditional local rankings reliably precedes improvement in Gemini local visibility.

Google Alerts for firm mentions. Set up Google Alerts for your firm name combined with each city you serve ("[Firm Name] + Hamilton," "[Firm Name] + Mississauga"). New mentions in local directories, news sources, community organizations, or legal publications contribute to the entity signals Gemini uses. Tracking mentions also helps you identify opportunities to get listed in sources you may have missed.

Timeline Expectations
Expect 3 to 6 months of consistent effort before seeing meaningful changes in Gemini local recommendations. GBP optimization and fresh reviews can show faster impact — sometimes within 4 to 8 weeks — particularly if your profile was previously incomplete. Content-based improvements take longer to be crawled, indexed, weighted, and reflected in Gemini's outputs. The firms that commit to this work consistently, and measure proxy signals along the way, are the ones that build durable local Gemini visibility over a 12 to 18 month horizon. The firms that wait until Gemini local search is unavoidable will find that catching up is harder and more expensive than getting started now.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Google Gemini use Google Maps data to recommend local lawyers?

Gemini draws on Google Business Profile data (which also powers Maps) but processes it differently. Maps ranks by proximity and rating. Gemini synthesizes multiple signals including your website content, review text, directory listings, and geographic content to make a recommendation it believes best matches the specific query — not just the nearest highly-rated option.

How does Gemini pick which lawyer to recommend for a specific local query?

Gemini matches the query's implied need (practice area + location + specific legal issue) against its aggregated knowledge of local firms. A firm with deep content about the specific legal issue, strong recent reviews mentioning that issue, and a well-optimized GBP listing will outperform a larger firm with thinner content and older reviews.

Does distance from the searcher matter for Gemini local recommendations?

Distance is one factor but not the dominant one. Gemini prioritizes matching the legal need to the right firm. An Ottawa family lawyer who has published deeply about Ontario parenting time disputes and has 40 recent five-star reviews mentioning custody cases will outperform a closer firm with weaker signals — especially for users who express a specific legal need rather than just "lawyer near me."

Can an Ontario law firm appear in Gemini recommendations for multiple cities?

Yes — if your firm legitimately serves multiple areas. The keys are: (a) have a GBP service area listing that includes all cities you serve, (b) create city-specific practice area pages on your website, (c) get reviews that mention different cities you serve, and (d) be listed in directories for each city. Avoid creating multiple GBP listings for the same location — this creates entity confusion and can backfire.

How long does it take to see results from optimizing for Gemini local search?

Expect 3 to 6 months of consistent effort before seeing meaningful changes in Gemini local recommendations. GBP optimization and fresh reviews can show faster impact (4-8 weeks). Content-based improvements take longer to be recognized and weighted. The firms that start now will have a substantial head start over competitors who wait until Gemini local search becomes unavoidable.
