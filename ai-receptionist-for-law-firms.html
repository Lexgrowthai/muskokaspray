Most law firms lose potential clients before the first conversation ever happens. A prospective client calls at 7:30 p.m. on a Thursday — maybe right after a car accident, maybe after being served divorce papers, maybe after getting arrested. They need to talk to someone. They don't get anyone. They call the next firm on the list.

This happens dozens of times a month at the average law firm. The problem isn't that attorneys aren't good at their jobs — it's that good attorneys are busy doing their jobs and can't staff a receptionist around the clock.

An AI receptionist for law firms solves that gap completely. It answers every call, day or night, weekend or holiday — speaks naturally with callers, asks the right qualifying questions, books consultations directly into your calendar, sends intake forms, and routes urgent matters appropriately. Your firm becomes reachable at exactly the moments prospective clients need you most.

The Problem

Why Law Firms Miss So Many Potential Clients

The legal industry has a specific call problem that most other service businesses don't face. Legal matters tend to be urgent. A car accident victim isn't going to wait three days to schedule a consultation at their convenience — they're calling multiple firms and retaining the first one that makes them feel heard.

The data on this is consistent across practice areas. Studies of legal intake show that:

  • The majority of potential clients contact multiple law firms before making a decision
  • Firms that respond within the first few minutes of initial contact win a significantly higher share of those clients
  • A substantial portion of all legal inquiries happen outside standard business hours — evenings and weekends
  • Most callers who reach voicemail don't leave a message and don't call back
The problem isn't that your firm lacks good lawyers. The problem is that the intake process has gaps — and every gap is a client retained by a competitor.

Staffing a human receptionist around the clock is not practical for most law firms. The cost alone — salaries, benefits, training, turnover — makes 24/7 human coverage economically unviable for all but the largest practices. An AI receptionist gives small and mid-size firms capabilities that previously only large firms could afford to consider.

How It Works

What Our AI Receptionist Actually Does

It's worth being specific, because this category has a wide range of quality. Some AI receptionist tools are little more than glorified voicemail. What we build for law firms is a genuine intake system.

Answers Every Incoming Call

Every call to your firm's main number is answered within two rings, any time of day or night. The caller hears a professional greeting specific to your firm — not a generic recording, not a menu system.

Qualifies Leads With Smart Questions

The AI asks qualifying questions specific to your practice areas. Type of matter, jurisdiction, what happened, timeline. Based on your firm's criteria, it categorises each caller and routes accordingly.

Books Consultations Directly

For qualified leads, the AI checks your calendar availability in real time and books the consultation directly — with confirmation sent to both the caller and your team. No back-and-forth scheduling.

Sends Intake Forms Automatically

After qualifying a caller, the system sends a customised intake form to their phone or email before the consultation — so your attorney has context before the first meeting and intake is partially complete.

Escalates Urgent Matters

When a caller indicates an urgent situation — arrested client, active emergency, critical deadline — the system flags the call and routes it to the appropriate attorney or emergency contact immediately.

Speaks Your Clients' Language

Bilingual support in English and Spanish is available for firms serving diverse client populations. The system detects language preference early in the conversation and adapts accordingly.

Practice Areas

Built For Every Practice Area

The qualifying questions your AI receptionist asks depend on the type of law you practice. A personal injury caller needs to describe the accident. A family law caller needs to explain whether there are children involved. A business law caller has different context needs entirely.

We configure the intake flow specifically for your practice areas. The AI doesn't ask a personal injury question to a probate caller. Every conversation is tailored to the matter type the caller presents.

Practice Area Specific Intake Flows

  • Personal Injury — Date of incident, type of accident, injuries sustained, other parties involved, insurance status, statute of limitations check
  • Family Law — Type of matter (divorce, custody, support), children involved, whether proceedings have started, urgency level
  • Criminal Defense — Charges or investigation type, jurisdiction, court date if any, urgency — with automatic escalation for imminent hearings
  • Business Law — Business type, matter type (contract, dispute, formation), timeline, counterparty information
  • Immigration — Matter type, current status, deadlines, bilingual intake available for Spanish-speaking clients
  • Wills & Estates — Nature of inquiry (new will, probate, dispute), existing documents, urgency based on health situation
What We See

Common Intake Mistakes Law Firms Make

✕

Relying On Voicemail As A Backup

Most callers who reach voicemail don't leave a message and don't call back. Voicemail is not a safety net — it's a lead-loss mechanism disguised as one.

✕

No After-Hours Coverage

Legal matters are not nine-to-five events. Car accidents, arrests, and family crises happen at all hours. Firms without after-hours coverage lose a disproportionate share of urgent, high-value matters.

✕

Intake Forms Completed At The Consultation

Having attorneys collect basic intake information during a paid consultation is an inefficient use of everyone's time. It also results in incomplete information if the client doesn't retain the firm.

✕

No Qualification Before Booking

Booking consultations for matters completely outside your practice areas wastes attorney time. A proper intake system screens for fit before a consultation is ever scheduled.

✕

Slow Follow-Up On Web Leads

Contact form submissions and chat inquiries that sit in an inbox for hours or days lose most of their potential. Speed of response to initial contact is a measurable factor in consultation conversion.

✕

One Phone Line For Everything

Existing clients checking on matters, prospective clients seeking consultations, and referrals from other attorneys all have different needs. A single undifferentiated phone line handles none of them optimally.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an AI receptionist for law firms?
An AI receptionist is a voice-based system that answers your firm's incoming calls 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. It greets callers, qualifies them as potential clients, collects basic case information, books consultations, and routes urgent matters to the appropriate attorney — without requiring a human staff member to be available.
Will callers know they're speaking to an AI?
The system speaks naturally and professionally, and most callers will experience it as a capable phone service. We recommend being straightforward if a caller directly asks — the AI is designed to handle this gracefully and offer to connect with a human if the caller prefers. Transparency is better than attempted deception, and most callers care far more about getting their question answered than about whether they spoke to a human.
Can an AI receptionist replace a human receptionist?
It handles the majority of routine call volume — new client inquiries, appointment scheduling, basic case screening, and after-hours calls. For complex existing client matters or sensitive conversations, the system routes calls to the appropriate attorney or staff member. Most firms use it to extend their coverage, not replace human staff entirely.
How does the AI receptionist qualify leads?
The system asks callers a set of qualifying questions specific to your practice areas — type of matter, jurisdiction, timeline, and basic facts. Based on your firm's criteria, it categorises the caller as a strong lead, a referral out, or a follow-up, and routes or logs accordingly.
What happens when someone calls with an emergency?
The AI is configured to recognise urgency indicators — an arrest, an imminent court date, a situation requiring immediate counsel. When these triggers are detected, the call is escalated immediately to your designated on-call attorney via call transfer and simultaneous text alert. No emergency gets routed to a scheduling queue.
Which practice management software does it integrate with?
We integrate with Clio, MyCase, Smokeball, Lawmatics, Google Calendar, Salesforce, HubSpot, and several others. If you use a system not on this list, we'll assess integration feasibility during onboarding. Most common legal practice management platforms are supported.
Can I customise the greeting and the questions it asks?
Yes, completely. The greeting uses your firm's name and reflects your firm's tone. The qualifying questions are built specifically for your practice areas and your criteria for a qualified client. Nothing is generic out of the box — it's configured for your firm specifically.
Does it work for multi-location firms?
Yes. We can configure separate intake flows for different office numbers, different practice groups, or different geographic locations — all managed within a single system. Routing logic can direct calls to the appropriate office or attorney based on the caller's matter type and location.
How long does setup take?
Most firms are live within two to three weeks. The timeline includes practice area mapping, call flow design, integration setup, and testing. We don't go live until the system has been thoroughly tested and you're satisfied with how it handles your firm's common call scenarios.
Can I listen to call recordings?
Yes. You have access to recordings and transcripts of all calls through your dashboard. We review these as part of ongoing optimisation, and you can review any call at any time.
Is this compliant with lawyer advertising rules?
The AI receptionist is a call answering and intake tool, not an advertising medium. It doesn't make claims about legal services or provide legal advice. It collects information and facilitates scheduling. We recommend reviewing the call flow with your state bar's ethics guidance if you have specific compliance questions — most bar associations have not identified AI receptionists as a regulated activity distinct from general receptionist services.
What happens to calls that don't qualify?
Calls that don't meet your qualification criteria are handled gracefully — the AI can explain that your firm focuses on different matter types, offer a referral if you have referral arrangements, or simply thank the caller and log the contact. You choose how non-qualified calls are handled.
Does it handle existing client calls too?
It can be configured to handle routine existing client inquiries — status updates, appointment confirmation, document requests — and route complex client matters to the appropriate attorney. For firms with high existing client call volume, this can meaningfully reduce staff time spent on routine calls.
What does it cost?
Pricing depends on call volume, the number of practice areas configured, and the integrations required. We structure it as a monthly service rather than a large upfront cost. The right way to evaluate it is to compare the cost against the value of one additional retained client per month — for most practice areas, the math is straightforward.
Can I try it before committing?
We offer a live demo where you can call a working example of the AI receptionist configured for a law firm similar to yours. Book a strategy call and we'll set that up so you can hear it in action before making any decision.
