Glossary

AI Search Glossary for Law Firms

Plain-English definitions of every term you need to understand AI search, legal SEO, and digital growth in 2026.

AI Search Optimization (AISO) New
The practice of structuring your website and content so that AI platforms like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity recommend your firm when potential clients ask legal questions. Builds on traditional SEO but requires additional focus on entity clarity, direct answers, and structured data.
Entity SEO
An SEO approach that helps search engines understand WHO your firm is, WHAT it does, and WHERE it operates — not just which keywords it targets. Involves schema markup, consistent NAP data, Knowledge Graph signals, and building a clear topical identity around your practice areas.
E-E-A-T Google Signal
Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. The four signals Google uses to evaluate legal content quality. For law firms, this means content attributed to qualified attorneys, verified credentials, client reviews, and citations to authoritative legal sources throughout the site.
Topical Authority
When your website covers a subject area so thoroughly that search engines and AI platforms recognize you as a comprehensive, credible source. For law firms, this means having in-depth content across all aspects of your practice areas — not just a single service page.
Schema Markup Technical
Structured code (JSON-LD) that tells search engines and AI platforms exactly what your website represents — your firm's name, location, services, attorneys, and more. Key schema types for law firms include LegalService, LocalBusiness, Attorney, FAQPage, and BreadcrumbList.
AI Overviews Google
Google's AI-generated summaries that appear at the top of many search results pages, synthesising an answer before users see the traditional list of links. For informational legal searches, AI Overviews frequently appear and reduce click-through rates to websites below.
Core Web Vitals Ranking Factor
Google's set of performance metrics that measure how quickly your page loads (LCP), how stable the layout is as it loads (CLS), and how quickly it responds to user input (FID/INP). All three are official Google ranking factors and directly impact how many visitors stay on your site.
NAP Consistency
Name, Address, and Phone number appearing identically across your website, Google Business Profile, legal directories, and every citation on the web. Inconsistent NAP data is one of the most common and damaging local SEO mistakes law firms make.
Knowledge Graph Google
Google's database of entities — people, businesses, places, and concepts — and the relationships between them. When your firm has a Knowledge Panel in Google Search results, it means Google has established your firm as a verified entity. Schema markup, consistent citations, and Wikipedia/Wikidata presence all help.
Perplexity AI AI Platform
An AI-powered search engine that cites its sources directly in responses. Law firms that appear as Perplexity citations gain both traffic and brand credibility. Perplexity pulls from web-indexed content and rewards sites with authoritative, well-structured information.
AI Receptionist
An AI-powered system that answers incoming calls to your law firm 24/7 — qualifying leads, answering common questions, and booking consultations without human involvement. Eliminates missed calls outside business hours, which is when most urgent legal inquiries occur.
Intake Automation
The use of AI tools to handle the client intake process from first contact through booked consultation — without requiring staff involvement at every step. Includes AI receptionists, chatbots, automated follow-up sequences, and calendar booking integrations.
Topical Silo
A content architecture strategy where a hub page covers a broad topic (e.g. Personal Injury Law) and links to a cluster of supporting articles covering subtopics (car accidents, slip and fall, medical malpractice). Siloing helps search engines understand your firm's expertise depth and improves rankings across the entire topic cluster.
Local Pack Google Maps
The three business listings that appear on Google Maps results for local searches (e.g. "family lawyer Toronto"). Appearing in the local pack requires an optimised Google Business Profile, consistent NAP data, positive reviews, and local schema markup on your website.
Conversion Rate
The percentage of website visitors who take a desired action — typically filling out a contact form or calling your firm. Most law firm websites convert 1–2% of visitors. A properly optimised legal website with strong trust signals and clear calls to action can reach 3–5%, doubling or tripling consultations from the same traffic.
Citation Building
The process of getting your firm's NAP data listed consistently across legal directories, general business directories, and other web properties. Strong citation profiles (Avvo, FindLaw, Justia, Yelp, Bing Places) support local SEO rankings and help AI platforms verify your firm's entity information.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is AI search optimization for law firms?
AI search optimization (AISO) is the practice of structuring your law firm's website and content so that AI platforms like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity recommend your firm when potential clients ask legal questions. It involves entity SEO, schema markup, topical authority, and E-E-A-T signals.
What is entity SEO for lawyers?
Entity SEO is an approach that helps search engines and AI platforms understand who your firm is, what it does, and where it operates — not just which keywords it targets. It involves schema markup, consistent NAP data, Google Business Profile optimization, and building a clear topical footprint around your practice areas.
How do I get my law firm to appear in ChatGPT?
To appear in ChatGPT responses, your firm needs a well-structured website with authoritative content, proper schema markup, strong E-E-A-T signals, and topical depth in your practice areas. ChatGPT pulls from web-indexed content, so the same foundations that help you rank on Google also help you appear in AI-generated answers.
What is topical authority for law firms?
Topical authority means your website covers a subject area so thoroughly that search engines and AI platforms recognize you as a credible, comprehensive source. For law firms, this means having in-depth content across all aspects of your practice areas — not just a single service page, but supporting articles, FAQs, guides, and local content.
What does E-E-A-T mean for law firm websites?
E-E-A-T stands for Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Google uses these four signals to evaluate legal content quality. For law firms, this means content attributed to qualified attorneys, clear credentials, verifiable results, client reviews, and citations to authoritative legal sources.
